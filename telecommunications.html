Common Telecom Questions

An MDU (multi-dwelling unit) telecom agreement is a contract between a property owner and a telecommunications provider governing access to a residential or commercial building for the purpose of delivering cable, internet, or phone services. These agreements define the terms of service, revenue sharing, marketing rights, and infrastructure access.
Yes. Community associations and HOAs often have significant leverage to negotiate bulk pricing, improved service levels, and revenue-sharing arrangements with broadband providers. Premier Law helps associations structure agreements that benefit residents while creating value for the community.
Key terms to evaluate include rental rates and escalation clauses, lease duration and renewal options, maintenance and repair obligations, insurance and liability provisions, equipment removal requirements, and structural impact assessments. An experienced telecom attorney can ensure these terms protect your property interests.
EV charging agreements for apartment communities typically cover equipment installation, utility costs, revenue sharing between the property owner and the charging provider, maintenance responsibilities, and resident access policies. Premier Law helps structure these agreements to maximize property value and resident satisfaction.
While not legally required, having a telecom attorney review bulk service contracts is strongly recommended. These agreements often span 5-15 years and involve significant financial commitments. An attorney can identify unfavorable terms, negotiate better rates, and ensure the contract protects your long-term interests.
A marketing agreement grants a telecom provider the right to market their services to residents of a property, often in exchange for marketing fees or revenue sharing. An access agreement grants physical access to the property for infrastructure installation and maintenance. Both can generate revenue for property owners, but they serve different purposes and carry different obligations.
Telecom agreements commonly range from 5 to 20 years depending on the type. Bulk service agreements typically run 5-10 years, while cell tower leases and rooftop antenna agreements can extend 15-25 years with renewal options. The length should reflect the investment required and the value delivered to both parties.
