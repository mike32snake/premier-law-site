Last Will and Testament

A will is the foundation of any estate plan. It outlines how your assets are distributed, names your heirs, designates guardians for minor children, and can include instructions for funeral arrangements.

Without a valid will, Florida intestacy laws determine who inherits your assets — which may not align with your wishes. Even a simple will provides critical protection and ensures your voice is heard.

Premier Law drafts wills tailored to your specific circumstances, whether you have a straightforward estate or complex holdings across multiple states.

What Happens Without a Will?

In Florida, dying without a will (intestate) means state law dictates how your assets are distributed. Your spouse, children, or other relatives may inherit in proportions you wouldn't choose. A valid will puts you in control.

Revocable & Irrevocable Trusts

Trusts are powerful estate planning tools that offer flexibility, privacy, and protection beyond what a will alone can provide.

Revocable Living Trusts

Maintain control of your assets during your lifetime while ensuring a smooth, private transfer upon death. Avoids probate entirely.

Irrevocable Trusts

Provide enhanced asset protection from creditors and lawsuits. Once established, assets are removed from your taxable estate.

Trust Administration

Guidance for trustees on managing trust assets, making distributions, and fulfilling fiduciary obligations.

A well-structured trust can save your family thousands in probate costs, keep your affairs private, and ensure your assets are managed according to your exact instructions.

Powers of Attorney

A power of attorney allows you to appoint someone you trust to manage your financial and personal affairs if you become unable to do so yourself. Without one, your family may need to go through costly and time-consuming guardianship proceedings.

Durable Financial Power of Attorney

Authorizes your agent to manage bank accounts, investments, real estate, and other financial matters.

Personal Power of Attorney

Covers non-financial decisions like living arrangements and daily care.

Premier Law ensures your power of attorney documents are properly drafted under Florida law, clearly define the scope of authority, and include appropriate safeguards.

Health Care Directives

Health care directives ensure your medical wishes are known and respected when you are unable to communicate them yourself.

Living Will

Documents your wishes regarding life-prolonging procedures, end-of-life care, and medical interventions.

Health Care Surrogate Designation

Names a trusted individual to make medical decisions on your behalf.

HIPAA Authorization

Allows designated individuals to access your medical records and communicate with healthcare providers.

Together, these documents provide comprehensive medical decision-making guidance that protects your autonomy and gives your family clarity during difficult times.

Guardianship Designations

For parents of minor children, naming a guardian is one of the most important estate planning decisions you will make. A guardianship designation ensures your children are cared for by someone you trust, according to your wishes.

Premier Law helps parents establish guardianship designations as part of a comprehensive estate plan, including standby guardianship provisions for immediate effect if needed.

Protecting Your Children

Without a guardianship designation, the court decides who cares for your children. Establishing a clear designation avoids uncertainty and ensures your family stays in trusted hands.

When to Update Your Estate Plan

Life changes often require updates to your estate plan. Consider reviewing your documents when you experience:

Marriage or Divorce

Birth or Adoption of a Child

Purchase of a Home or Significant Asset

Starting or Selling a Business

Moving to a New State

Death of a Beneficiary or Named Agent

Significant Change in Financial Circumstances

Retirement

Premier Law recommends reviewing your estate plan every 3–5 years or whenever a major life event occurs.
